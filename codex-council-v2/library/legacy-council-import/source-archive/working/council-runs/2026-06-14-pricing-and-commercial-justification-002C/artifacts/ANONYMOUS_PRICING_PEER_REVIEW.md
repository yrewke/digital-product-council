# Anonymous Pricing Peer Review

## Review 1

- **Strongest insight:** Responsibility-based pricing is more trustworthy than ROI-led pricing.
- **Weakest assumption:** Monthly support use of 0.5/1/2 hours is unmeasured and may not justify recurring minimums.
- **Price too high:** T0 recurring AED 250-500 may be too high for a simple-page perception.
- **Price too low:** T2 launch near AED 6,750 is unsafe against workflow effort and risk.
- **Missed objection:** Why pay setup plus monthly when staff still maintain menu, POS, fulfilment, and aggregators?
- **Missed support:** Staff turnover, retraining, menu coordination, and peak troubleshooting.
- **Missed hosting:** Missed-order consequences, outage communications, response expectations, and exit.
- **Module correction:** Include basic WhatsApp handoff in T1/T2 where trivial; keep advanced messaging/payment/delivery behind discovery.
- **Proposed correction:** T0 fixed setup plus optional care; T1 includes basic handoff; T2 has higher qualified floor and mandatory managed support.

## Review 2

- **Strongest insight:** Separate provider responsibility from setup, pass-through costs, and exceptional work.
- **Weakest assumption:** Support use excludes incidents, vendor coordination, retraining, and difficult-client variance.
- **Price too high:** T0 recurring against one-time sites, WhatsApp, and low-cost SaaS.
- **Price too low:** Lower T2 setup can fall below modeled delivery cost.
- **Missed objection:** Existing POS/SaaS may already provide a hosted operational queue.
- **Missed support:** Offboarding, export, billing, collection, cancellation, security maintenance, vendor escalation, and rescue.
- **Missed hosting:** Restoration, outage response, billing failure, security, transfer timing, and migration.
- **Module correction:** Payment is unsafe as a broad fixed-range module.
- **Proposed correction:** Fixed prices only for qualified finite scopes; paid discovery for T2 exceptions, payment, delivery rules, and responsibility-heavy bots.

## Review 3

- **Strongest insight:** Infrastructure cost is not the main issue; unpriced responsibility is.
- **Weakest assumption:** Monthly support forecasts exclude acquisition, account management, incident coordination, retention, and negotiation.
- **Price too high:** T0 recurring AED 350-500 without visible ongoing outcomes.
- **Price too low:** T2 below AED 9,000 setup and AED 1,100/month.
- **Missed objection:** Mature SaaS/POS can bundle queues, updates, payment capabilities, and support with little setup.
- **Missed support:** Qualification, renewal, offboarding, and triage.
- **Missed hosting:** Monitoring, backups, security response, restoration, vendor outages, DNS/domain, after-hours.
- **Module correction:** WhatsApp bots, payment, and delivery rules are premature fixed-price modules.
- **Proposed correction:** T2 standard floor near AED 12,000 setup and AED 1,100/month until measured data supports reductions.

## Council-Level Corrections

1. T0 recurring support is optional unless the provider retains managed responsibility.
2. T1 recurring support is optional for client-owned/static responsibility and mandatory for managed service.
3. T2 managed support is mandatory; its launch price must not fall below the modeled cost floor.
4. Use fixed prices only for qualified finite scopes.
5. Basic WhatsApp handoff may be included in T1/T2 when it is merely a link/handoff; automation remains gated.
6. Payment, delivery rules, deterministic bots, integrations, AI, and high-variance operations require discovery or scope-triggered quotes.
7. Hosting and support responsibility matrices must define monitoring, backup, restoration, outage, security, migration, billing, export, and after-hours boundaries.
8. The first cohort must log acquisition, setup, cleanup, revisions, training, support, incidents, vendor coordination, offboarding, and collection.

## Preserved Disagreement

- Whether T0 should ever carry a mandatory recurring fee.
- Whether T1 managed support creates enough visible value for the proposed monthly price.
- Whether a standardized T2 can safely receive a fixed public range before implementation-time data exists.
- Whether simple delivery rules can become a fixed module after a data gate or should remain discovery-priced.
