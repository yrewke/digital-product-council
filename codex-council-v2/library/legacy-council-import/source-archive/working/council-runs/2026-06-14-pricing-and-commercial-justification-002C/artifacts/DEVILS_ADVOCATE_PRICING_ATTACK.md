# Devil's Advocate Pricing Attack

## Core Attack

The direction may be too expensive and complicated for disciplined restaurants, but too cheap and bounded for operationally difficult restaurants.

Restaurants hear: pay setup, pay support, keep paying existing vendors, keep staff responsible for orders and fulfilment, and pay separately for changes, incidents, integrations, and after-hours help. A responsibility matrix clarifies exclusions but does not create buyer value.

## Why Alternatives Win

- **Cheap WordPress:** Enough for presence, menu, map, and WhatsApp button.
- **Manual WhatsApp:** Already installed, flexible, familiar, and cheap in visible cash terms.
- **POS/SaaS:** May already provide one integrated queue, payments, updates, support, and lower supplier risk.
- **Aggregators:** Preserve discovery and fulfilment convenience.
- **Doing nothing:** Avoids change, training, and another queue.

## Prices Too High

- T0 managed first year: AED 7,800 builder fees.
- T1 managed first year: AED 15,300 builder fees.
- T2 managed first year: AED 28,200 builder fees before discovery, pass-through, modules, VAT, and overages.

The buyer still owns menu accuracy, monitoring, fulfilment, POS use, complaints, refunds, and aggregators.

## Prices Too Low

- T2 modeled cost floor reaches AED 13,100 before acquisition, account management, incidents, collection, and offboarding.
- T2 launch at AED 12,000 may already lose money.
- AED 1,100/month may not cover incidents, retraining, vendor escalation, security coordination, and peak rescue.
- T0/T1 margin can vanish through cleanup, input chasing, revisions, and acceptance disputes.

## Support And Hosting Failure

Monthly support is rejected when visible output is unclear and exclusions are extensive. One-time pricing creates long-tail unpaid diagnosis. Managed hosting creates an expectation gap during incidents. Client-owned hosting adds friction without reliably transferring blame or support burden.

## Module And Launch Risks

Modules create uncertainty, longer sales cycles, and perceived nickel-and-diming. Launch discounts attract discount-led, high-change, weak-process clients and can anchor future pricing.

## Adverse Selection

Restaurants with the greatest apparent need may have the worst data, highest turnover, weakest discipline, and lowest willingness to pay. Disciplined restaurants may already have adequate POS/SaaS.

## Neglected Alternative

Sell a narrow paid readiness audit and implementation service that may recommend WhatsApp, existing POS/SaaS, cheap website, or custom direct channel. This can create evidence before committing to a proprietary responsibility-heavy service.

## Failure Scenario

Several discounted clients create cleanup, discovery expansion, missed-order disputes, urgent diagnosis, retraining, vendor help, overage disputes, delayed new work, cancellations, and no viable recurring margin.

## Evidence That Defeats The Attack

Qualified restaurants choose and retain the offer at proposed prices; can name the responsibility removed; paid T2 discovery bounds work; setup/support/incidents fit pricing; exclusions are accepted; launch clients convert to standard pricing; and measured value or burden removal is demonstrated.
