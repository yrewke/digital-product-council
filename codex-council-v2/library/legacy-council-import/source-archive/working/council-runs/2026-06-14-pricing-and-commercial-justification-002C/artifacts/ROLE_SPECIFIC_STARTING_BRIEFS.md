# Role-Specific Starting Briefs

All roles must price the Run 2B package gate, preserve aggregator coexistence, separate pass-through costs, and avoid guaranteed ROI.

## 1. Restaurant Owner Buyer
Test whether each fee is understandable against doing nothing, WhatsApp, cheap WordPress, POS/SaaS, and aggregator coexistence. Reject confusing or trust-damaging fee structures.

## 2. Restaurant Cashflow Analyst
Protect restaurant cashflow and provider collection. Compare deposits, launch discounts, monthly burden, annual prepay, cancellation, and no-break-even cases.

## 3. ROI Pricing Analyst
Use measured owner data to bound willingness to pay. Do not price only from avoided commission or monetize trust/SEO automatically.

## 4. Competitor Price Analyst
Compare unlike alternatives honestly by job, responsibility, recurring cost, support, data ownership, and upgrade path.

## 5. Cheap WordPress Objection Analyst
State when AED 300 brochure work is enough and when structured ordering/workflow responsibility is a different job.

## 6. Packaging and Offer Analyst
Keep three base choices understandable. Hide complexity behind gated modules and discovery rather than a sprawling public menu.

## 7. Hosting and Responsibility Analyst
Price account ownership, monitoring, backup, vendor billing, outage handling, exit, and migration explicitly.

## 8. Developer Cost and Support Analyst
Build cost floors from setup, content, revisions, training, support, incidents, maintenance, continuity, collection, and scope risk.

## 9. Third-Party Cost Analyst
Keep vendor costs transparent and changeable. Never bury messaging, AI, gateway, POS, delivery, domain, or infrastructure costs.

## 10. Sales Practicality Analyst
Prefer prices and payment structures explainable in one meeting. Identify what can be sold this week without creating false expectations.

## 11. Evidence Auditor
Downgrade fake precision, unsupported market anchors, vendor claims, unmeasured hours, and ROI-dependent prices.

## 12. Devil's Advocate
Attack high prices, low prices, recurring support, one-time-only support exposure, managed liability, client-owned friction, and bad-client attraction.

## 13. Chairman
Choose launch and standard internal prices, recurring structures, allowances, modules, hosting options, custom-quote gates, and preserved dissent.
