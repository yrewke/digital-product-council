# Executive Pricing Memos

These are sealed first-round recommendations. They are independent opinions, not the council verdict. All prices exclude third-party costs and tax.

## Memo A - Restaurant Owner Buyer / Cashflow / Sales Practicality

**Verdict:** Conditional support for setup plus clearly bounded recurring support.

| Tier | Launch setup | Standard setup | Managed monthly |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| T0 | AED 1,900-2,600 | AED 2,500-3,500 | AED 250-350 |
| T1 | AED 3,400-4,900 | AED 4,500-6,500 | AED 450-650 |
| T2 | AED 6,750-10,500 | AED 9,000-14,000 | AED 750-1,100 |

Use a named launch cohort with approximately 25% setup discount, 50% deposit, finite allowances, transparent pass-through costs, and no long installment plans. Managed hosting is the default for T2; client-owned adds AED 500-1,500 setup.

Cheap WordPress is enough for a brochure need. The restaurant-specific offer is justified only where structured order capture, workflow responsibility, training, support, and an upgrade path are needed.

**Biggest risk:** Staff fail to monitor or operate the system. Refuse bad-fit sales rather than discount.

## Memo B - Restaurant Operator / Hosting / Third-Party Cost

**Verdict:** Conditional support; operational reliability must not be confused with software availability.

| Tier | Launch setup | Standard setup | Managed monthly | Client-owned monthly |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| T0 | AED 1,900-2,600 | AED 2,500-3,500 | AED 250-350 | AED 200-300 |
| T1 | AED 3,400-4,900 | AED 4,500-6,500 | AED 450-650 | AED 350-500 |
| T2 | AED 6,750-10,100 | AED 9,000-13,500 | AED 750-1,100 | AED 650-900 |

Provider-managed hosting should be the launch default. Client-owned or migration adds AED 500-1,500. Product defects do not consume support allowances; restaurant-requested changes, retraining, content, and operational troubleshooting do.

Suggested module ranges: WhatsApp 1 AED 350-700; WhatsApp 2 AED 2,000-4,000 plus AED 250-500/month; Telegram 2 AED 1,500-3,000 plus AED 200-400/month; catering AED 700-1,500; payment AED 1,500-3,500 plus AED 200-500/month; delivery rules AED 1,000-2,500 plus AED 150-350/month. Integrations and AI remain custom/delayed.

**Biggest risk:** Peak-hour staff discipline creates rescue support and blame transfer.

## Memo C - ROI Pricing / Restaurant Cashflow / Evidence Economics

**Verdict:** Price responsibility and sustainability first; use ROI only as an owner-specific affordability test.

| Tier | Launch setup | Standard setup | Managed monthly |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| T0 | AED 1,900-2,250 | AED 2,500-3,000 | AED 250-350 |
| T1 | AED 3,750-4,500 | AED 5,000-6,000 | AED 450-600 |
| T2 | AED 7,900-9,400 | AED 10,500-12,500 | AED 750-1,000 |

Launch discounts apply to setup only. T0/T1 payment: 60% deposit and 40% after acceptance. T2: 50% deposit, 30% after workflow sign-off, 20% after acceptance. Do not offer annual discounts until retention and support consumption are measured.

Do not automatically monetize trust, SEO, customer data, loyalty, or basket uplift. Numeric module prices are unsafe until module delivery hours are measured.

**Biggest risk:** Prices appear justified by ROI assumptions that may fail after fulfilment, promotion, payment, and support costs.

| Tier | Launch setup | Standard setup | Launch monthly | Standard monthly |

| T0 | AED 1,900-2,600 | AED 2,500-3,500 | AED 250-350 | AED 300-450 |

**Biggest risk:** T2 loses to POS/SaaS where an existing operational queue already solves the job; T0 monthly fees face one-time-website expectations.

## Memo E - Developer Cost / Support / One-Developer Profitability

**Verdict:** The largest threat is unpriced onboarding, content cleanup, revisions, training, incidents, vendor coordination, and custom requests.

**Assumed cost floors:** AED 175 productive hour plus 15% buffer.

| Tier | Buffered cost floor | Launch setup | Standard setup | Launch monthly | Standard monthly |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| T0 | AED 2,000-3,250 | AED 2,400-3,200 | AED 3,200-4,200 | AED 250-350 | AED 350-500 |
| T1 | AED 4,000-6,500 | AED 4,800-6,500 | AED 6,500-8,500 | AED 450-650 | AED 650-850 |
| T2 | AED 8,000-13,100 | AED 9,000-12,000 | AED 12,000-16,000 | AED 750-1,100 | AED 1,100-1,500 |

Recommended overage rate is AED 275/hour standard, AED 225-250/hour for launch cohort exceptional work, and 1.5x only under a separate after-hours agreement. Client-owned infrastructure still requires recurring product support.

Fixed early modules: WhatsApp handoff AED 500-1,000; catering AED 750-1,500; language/content structure AED 1,200-2,500. Payment, delivery rules, bots, integrations, AI, multi-branch, and advanced operations require stronger gates or paid discovery.

**Biggest risk:** Lower sales-friendly prices fall below actual cost when delivery approaches the high effort estimate.

## Shared Conclusions

- Separate setup, recurring support, third-party pass-through, and exceptional work.
- Use finite allowances and paid overages.
- Limit launch pricing to a named validation cohort.
- Do not discount recurring responsibility heavily.
- Managed hosting is the launch default; client-owned adds setup and preserves support responsibility.
- Do not guarantee ROI or claim aggregator replacement.
- Recommend cheap WordPress, SaaS, POS, WhatsApp, aggregators, or doing nothing where they solve the job better.

## Material Disagreement

The lower ranges in Memos A-D improve buyer accessibility but can fall below the high-end cost floors in Memo E. The council must decide whether standard fixed prices should use a narrow qualified-scope midpoint, wider quoted ranges, or explicit scope-based price movement.

## Evidence Gap

All numeric prices remain scenario assumptions until actual setup time, support use, incidents, close reasons, and retention are measured.
