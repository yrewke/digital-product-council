# Devil's Advocate ROI Attack

**Emerging direction attacked:** An owner-data scenario calculator and smallest-reversible owned-channel test.

## Why ROI May Not Materialize

- The restaurant's “repeat customers” may still prefer aggregators for convenience, offers, payment, tracking, or support.
- Direct orders may merely cannibalize already-profitable WhatsApp, phone, pickup, or POS-native orders.
- The restaurant may need discounts and promotion spending that erase avoided marketplace deductions.
- Delivery, refunds, failed handoffs, and staff exceptions may consume more margin than the model captures.
- The system may create a second queue that staff ignore during peak hours.
- Menu accuracy, availability, and fulfilment discipline may fail, damaging trust instead of building it.

## Why Trust May Not Convert

A polished website can increase legitimacy without generating a single order. Customers may use it only to view the menu before ordering elsewhere. Google and social traffic may remain low or non-commercial. Credibility is not a causal revenue engine by itself.

## Why Manual WhatsApp May Be Good Enough

For low-volume, relationship-heavy, or highly customized orders, manual WhatsApp can be flexible, familiar, and effectively free. The proposed structure may remove useful conversation while adding setup, maintenance, and monitoring.

## Why Delivery Can Destroy Margin

The direct channel inherits dispatch, waiting, redelivery, customer support, and refund responsibility. A low AOV or broad radius can make every direct delivery economically worse than an aggregator order even when commission is avoided.

## Why the Calculator Can Mislead

- Owners may accept prefilled assumptions as predictions.
- Actual contribution margin and marketplace deductions are often misunderstood.
- Shifted and incremental orders can be double-counted.
- Staff-time “savings” may never reduce payroll.
- High scenarios can dominate sales conversations.
- Missing support, failure, and promotion costs can make a weak case look positive.

## Why the Offer May Attract Low-Quality Clients

Restaurants with poor records, weak operations, stale menus, low demand, and unrealistic expectations may be most attracted to a promised digital fix. They are also most likely to require rescue work, dispute results, churn, and consume support.

## Evidence That Would Defeat This Attack

Multiple pilots showing source-tagged profitable direct activity, reliable peak operation, lower measured leakage, accepted support boundaries, positive restaurant net contribution, and viable provider contribution against the best alternative.
