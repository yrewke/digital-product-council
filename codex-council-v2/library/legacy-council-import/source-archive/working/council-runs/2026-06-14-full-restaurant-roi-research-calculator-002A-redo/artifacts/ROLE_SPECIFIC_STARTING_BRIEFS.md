# Role-Specific Starting Briefs

**Shared question:** What realistic ways can a UAE restaurant gain, save, protect, or risk money from a website/direct-ordering/WhatsApp/Telegram owned-channel system, and how should those paths become a customer-facing ROI calculator?

**Shared constraints:** No final tiers, public prices, sales copy, guarantees, aggregator-replacement claim, universal POS promise, or early delivery-API promise. Bounded numbers are required. Preserve dissent.

## 1. Restaurant Owner Economist

Evaluate the decision as an owner protecting cash. Focus on willingness to pay, monthly cash effect, payback period, downside, hidden obligations, trust, and whether the system creates confidence or another bill. Identify the minimum data and evidence that makes an owner say yes, wait, or no.

## 2. Restaurant Operator / Fulfilment Manager

Trace the order from customer intent to kitchen, pickup, driver/rider, complaint, refund, and closure. Quantify avoidable minutes, errors, missed messages, duplicate entry, peak pressure, and exception burden. Reject ROI that merely moves work elsewhere.

## 3. Marketing and Trust Analyst

Assess website credibility, Google and social link destinations, menu clarity, legitimacy, shareability, QR use, and catering/reservation enquiry capture. Separate measurable lead/order paths from trust hypotheses. Define proxy measures without pretending trust always converts.

## 4. Direct-Order Economics Analyst

Model direct volume, AOV, gross margin, marketplace fees, repeat demand, incentives, payment fees, fulfilment, and break-even. Distinguish incremental orders from shifted orders. Prevent gross-sales savings from being mislabeled as net profit.

## 5. Delivery and Fulfilment Analyst

Compare pickup, existing driver, hired rider, motorcycle only, motorcycle plus rider, outsourced per-order delivery, API provider, and hybrid zones. Include failed-delivery/refund risk, idle capacity, density, radius, and service reliability.

## 6. Channel Analyst

Compare website ordering, WhatsApp handoff, deterministic WhatsApp bot, WhatsApp AI assistant, Telegram receiver, deterministic Telegram bot, Telegram AI assistant, and hybrid stack. Evaluate adoption friction, transaction safety, operating cost, support, and channel fit.

## 7. Competitor and Alternatives Analyst

Compare the proposed system against manual WhatsApp/phone, Instagram-only, Google Maps only, QR menus, agencies, ordering SaaS, POS modules, aggregators, and doing nothing. Determine when the owned system wins, loses, or duplicates an adequate existing tool.

## 8. Developer Cost and Support Analyst

Estimate setup/build/support burden, hosting, maintenance, tokens, API charges, vendor fees, customization, and continuity risk. Identify features whose customer ROI may be positive but provider support economics are dangerous for one developer.

## 9. ROI Calculator Designer

Turn defensible value paths into understandable owner inputs, formulas, conservative/base/optimistic scenarios, warnings, and recommendations. Keep inputs short enough to complete, allow advanced editing, and make uncertainty visible.

## 10. Evidence Auditor

Challenge source quality, geography, fake precision, vendor claims, missing costs, causal leaps, and unsafe defaults. Mark what can be stated as fact, editable benchmark, scenario assumption, hypothesis, or excluded from monetary output.

## 11. Devil's Advocate

Build the strongest case that ROI will fail: direct orders do not materialize, trust does not convert, manual WhatsApp is good enough, delivery destroys margin, staff ignore the workflow, calculator assumptions are manipulated, and low-quality clients consume support.

## 12. Chairman

Synthesize without averaging away disagreement. Decide the calculator structure, strongest and weakest ROI stories, archetype priorities, allowed/default assumptions, warnings, and exact Run 2B handoff. Be decisive without finalizing public prices.
