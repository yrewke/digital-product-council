# Packaging Final Verdict

## 1. Executive Summary

Choose a **hybrid responsibility-based packaging model with three base packages and a finite gated module catalog**.

The three base packages are:

1. Tier 0 - Ordering Page: captures structured orders.
2. Tier 1 - Website + Ordering: provides a clear owned destination plus order capture.
3. Tier 2 - Direct Order Workflow: provides an accountable staff queue and status workflow.

Do **not** create a fixed Tier 3 bundle. Commerce, channels, fulfilment rules, payments, analytics, and supported integrations become separately gated modules. Validation is a standardized launch motion attached to a real package, not a substitute product.

## 2. Chosen Packaging Model

**Confirmed decision:** hybrid base packages plus paid modules, with custom quotes only for high-variance integrations and operations.

## 3. Why This Model Was Chosen

- Fixed tiers alone are clear but trap irrelevant features and implied support.
- Pure feature pricing maps to jobs but confuses owners and recreates bespoke work.
- Hybrid packages define responsibility clearly while modules serve measured optional jobs.
- Validation-first is useful as a launch method but too weak as the only product.

This is a bounded council decision, not proven buyer preference or provider profitability.

## 4. Smallest Sellable Package

**Tier 0 is selected as the smallest sellable package**, conditional on existing demand, a named restaurant monitoring owner, clear fallback, and accepted responsibility.

Its technical scope is locked. Its sellability remains a market-test hypothesis.

## 5. Tier 0 Confirmation

Confirm unchanged: logo/simple header, one menu/cart ordering page, customer details/notes, Telegram order receiver, local SQLite history; no auth, dashboard, payment, POS, delivery API, table ordering, or advanced analytics.

Add no hidden capability. Before sale, define monitoring, missed-message fallback, data backup/export/device failure, domain/hosting, support, and exit responsibilities.

## 6. Tier 1 Recommendation

Retain **Website + Ordering** as a distinct base package only for the measured job of providing a clear web/social/Google/QR destination.

Include template-based home page, contact, hours, location/map link, social links, QR route, basic structured information, and ordering. Move catering, languages, custom content, photography, and managed updates to modules/allowances.

Do not position Tier 1 as an automatic upgrade or revenue/SEO generator.

## 7. Tier 2 Recommendation

Confirm **Direct Order Workflow** as the clearest differentiated package and the smallest operationally credible option for meaningful direct-order volume.

Include auth/dashboard, one accountable queue, accept/reject/status workflow, order history, availability/menu controls, and approved pickup/manual-delivery details.

Require named shift ownership, workflow sign-off, bounded training, support cap, and peak-period test. Manual POS entry and restaurant fulfilment remain restaurant responsibilities.

## 8. Tier 3 Recommendation

**Reject a fixed Tier 3 bundle for now.** Replace it with individually gated commerce modules after Tier 2 or where dependencies permit.

Payment, delivery zones/fees/minimums, customer records, promotions, deeper analytics, and notifications have materially different responsibilities and support risks. Price and approve them separately.

## 9. Future Tier 4/Tier 5 / Custom Boundary

Kitchen view, advanced roles, multi-branch, advanced reporting, multiple delivery providers, own-driver dispatch, webhooks, complex integrations, and migrations are delayed/custom.

Inventory, recipes, procurement, ERP, aggregator middleware, and enterprise operations are outside current standard product scope.

## 11. Channel Add-On Policy

The base deterministic menu/cart/order record is the source of truth. Website ordering is the stable base door. WhatsApp and Telegram customer channels are optional modules. Telegram receiver is a staff notification in Tier 0.

Deterministic bots require measured channel volume and bounded intents. AI assistants are custom/delayed and may talk, explain, recommend, translate, and guide, but may not own transaction facts or final confirmation.

## 12. POS Integration Policy

No standard or universal POS integration. Classify each POS as supported, conditional, custom/expensive, avoid, or unknown. A “supported” adapter requires access, test, acceptance, support boundary, maintenance plan, and failure policy.

## 13. Delivery Integration Policy

No early default delivery API. Tier 2 may capture approved manual fulfilment details. Delivery rules and supported provider integrations are modules requiring complete fulfilment economics, compatibility, responsibility, and pass-through cost disclosure.

## 14. Payment Policy

Payment is a separately priced, conditional module. Require merchant/gateway account readiness, fee disclosure, reconciliation, refund/chargeback/fraud policy, outage fallback, and support boundary.

## 15. Catering / Enquiry Module Policy

Offer as an early add-on for family-tray, catering, event, office, and group-order prospects with a named follow-up owner and defined enquiry fields. Sell capture capability, not guaranteed wins.

## 16. Table QR Policy

Conditional add-on only after the dine-in/table workflow is documented and staff accepts the process. Do not bundle into Tier 2 by default.

## 17. Dashboard / Status Workflow Policy

Core Tier 2 responsibility boundary. It must create one accountable direct-order queue, not another ignored dashboard. Require training, shift ownership, statuses, availability discipline, and escalation/fallback.

## 18. Analytics / Source-Tracking Policy

Keep basic source tags and order outcomes as a measurement capability. Deeper analytics is a module or Tier 2/commerce extension only when defined decisions use it. Do not sell generic dashboards as ROI.

## 19. Features to Refuse Now

- Universal POS/delivery integration
- Unlimited customization, revisions, content, training, or support
- Provider ownership of restaurant fulfilment, food quality, complaints, or refund decisions
- Guaranteed ROI, conversion, revenue, savings, SLA, reliability, SEO, or ranking
- Aggregator replacement claims
- ERP/inventory/procurement/recipe scope in standard packages

## 20. Features to Delay

AI assistants, kitchen view, multi-branch, advanced reporting, aggregator middleware, complex automation, multiple delivery providers, and advanced operations.

## 21. Features Requiring Restaurant Data Gate

Payment economics, delivery zones/rules, catering ROI, promotions, bots, table QR, customer records, analytics, POS/delivery integration, and any recommendation based on volume/leakage.

## 22. Features Requiring Provider Support Gate

Tier 2 workflow, payments, notifications, bots, AI, integrations, multi-branch, managed content, custom automation, and after-hours support.

## 23. Third-Party Pass-Through Cost Policy

Separate builder setup/support price from domains, hosting, messages, BSP, AI tokens, gateway/dispute/refund fees, POS/API fees, delivery fees, maps, analytics, SMS, and vendor charges. Restaurant pays directly or receives transparent pass-through billing and budgets.

## 24. Responsibility Boundaries

Every package/module must state exact inclusions, allowances, exclusions, restaurant dependencies, acceptance test, named owners, support limit, fallback, data/privacy/backup/export/deletion/exit, incident, vendor failure, and exceptional-work policy.

## 25. Candidate Archetype Fit

- Tier 0: low-volume disciplined restaurants with existing direct demand.
- Tier 1: no/weak-website, Instagram/Google/QR-driven, family/group-enquiry prospects.
- Tier 2: WhatsApp-heavy, pickup/manual-delivery, higher-volume restaurants with measurable leakage.
- Modules: only after their job/data gate is proven.

These are recruitment hypotheses, not proven fit claims.

## 26. Archetypes Not to Sell Each Package

- Do not sell Tier 0 where no one will monitor orders or demand is absent.
- Do not sell Tier 1 where a simple QR/POS/SaaS/agency solution already solves the job better.
- Do not sell Tier 2 where staff will not own statuses/availability or a capable POS-native queue is superior.
- Do not sell commerce/integration modules when costs, compatibility, responsibility, or provider capacity are unknown.

## 27. Demo to Build First

Build the Tier 0 live-order demo first: customer opens link, selects menu/cart, submits complete details, restaurant receives Telegram notification, and order appears in history.

Then build a Tier 2 workflow demo showing acknowledgement, accept/reject, availability, statuses, pickup/manual delivery details, and traceable outcome.

## 28. What Can Be Sold This Week

Sell a **qualified Tier 0 Ordering Page** with explicit scope, owner responsibilities, setup/support/pass-through separation, and time-bounded launch measurement. Offer Tier 1 only where the destination job is observed. Offer WhatsApp 1 or catering enquiry as bounded add-ons where relevant.

Do not sell unbuilt or unqualified Tier 2/commerce/integration promises.

## 29. What Must Wait for Run 2C Pricing

All setup prices, recurring support prices/allowances, module prices, validation/launch terms, exceptional-work rates, custom-quote rules, pass-through administration, discounts, payment terms, and price presentation.

## 30. What Must Wait for Pilot Data

Buyer acceptance, Tier 0 sellability, Tier 1 upgrade value, Tier 2 operational improvement, module demand, archetype fit, support acceptance, onboarding effort, monthly support load, incidents, customization, retention, and provider profitability.

## Final Decision

Proceed to Run 2C with three base packages plus gated modules. Price Tier 0, Tier 1, Tier 2, common modules, recurring support, launch validation, exceptional work, and custom integrations separately. Preserve the right to recommend WhatsApp, POS/SaaS, aggregators, or doing nothing instead.
