# Assumption Ledger

| ID | Assumption | Confidence | Why it matters | What would prove it wrong |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A-001 | Owners understand three responsibility-based packages better than a feature menu. | Medium-low | Determines ladder structure. | Sales calls show repeated confusion or preference for feature-by-feature buying. |
| A-002 | Tier 0 is sellable as a real product when existing demand and staff ownership exist. | Low-medium | Determines smallest sellable package. | Qualified restaurants reject it as too incomplete or fail to use it. |
| A-003 | Optional modules can preserve flexibility without recreating custom agency work. | Medium | Determines hybrid approach. | Module combinations create high variance, support, or quoting confusion. |
| A-004 | A bounded launch/validation motion improves sales honesty without making the product sound unreal. | Medium | Determines go-to-market motion. | Buyers consistently view validation as unfinished software. |
| A-005 | Tier 2 dashboard/status workflow creates enough operational clarity to justify greater responsibility. | Medium-low | Determines Tier 2 boundary. | Pilot shows second-queue burden remains or owners do not value statuses. |
