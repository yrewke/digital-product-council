## Assumptions Made

- Pickup-only is the cleanest low-burden baseline.
- Existing-driver delivery is more attractive than hiring new owned fleet capacity.
- Hired riders and motorcycle-based delivery need stable delivery density.
- WhatsApp/manual ordering is a real alternative but becomes brittle with growth.
- Aggregators remain discovery engines, not replacement targets.

## ROI Verdict

Positive ROI exists, but only under bounded conditions:

- Pickup-only with existing demand.
- Existing staff or driver handling delivery.
- Outsourced delivery where own fleet is not justified.
- Hybrid fulfilment with clear zone rules.
- Direct-channel repeat capture from aggregator-heavy restaurants that already have owned traffic.

Negative or weak cases:

- Low-volume restaurants.
- Manual WhatsApp-only operations at scale.
- Motorcycle-only ownership.
- Restaurants with no repeat traffic and no disciplined fulfilment.

## Fulfilment Verdict

The best practical fulfilment ladder is:

1. Pickup only.
2. Existing staff/driver delivery.
3. Outsourced delivery.
4. Hybrid.
5. Hire rider or motorcycle-plus-rider only after volume proves out.

## Best First Test

Pickup-only on a neighborhood QSR, fast casual, or family-tray/catering-led restaurant with repeat demand and a compact radius.

## Worst Traps

- Treating aggregator dominance as a replacement target.
- Scaling manual WhatsApp ordering without a structure.
- Buying fleet assets before delivery density is proven.
- Using broad sales claims instead of restaurant-specific economics.
- Turning the offer into a custom service stack.
