# Fulfilment Model Matrix

| Model | Cost type | Fixed cost | Variable cost | Operational burden | Risk | Best-fit restaurant | Minimum demand signal | Recommended action |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pickup only | Mostly fixed-light | Low | Low | Low if menu and handoff are disciplined | Low customer friction, but weak demand if pickup is not habitual | Neighborhood QSR, lunch spots, offices, convenience-led concepts | Existing pickup demand or strong local traffic | Test first when pickup already exists |
| Existing staff or existing restaurant driver handles delivery | Mixed | Low to medium | Medium | Medium; scheduling and accountability matter | Staff distraction, inconsistent timing | Small restaurants with a reliable in-house driver or slow delivery density | Consistent delivery volume in a small radius | Good bridge model before hiring or outsourcing |
| Restaurant hires a rider | Mostly fixed | Medium to high | Low to medium | Medium to high; adds HR and shift management | Idle time risk if demand is uneven | Stable delivery restaurants with predictable volumes | Enough daily deliveries to cover labor and compliance | Only if volume is repeatable |
| Motorcycle only | Mostly fixed asset/lease | Medium | Low | Medium; requires a rider or driver anyway | Asset underutilization | Same as rider-based models with route density | High daily route density | Rarely useful alone |
| Motorcycle plus rider | Mostly fixed | High | Low to medium | High; needs dispatch discipline | Highest idle-capacity risk if demand swings | Dense local delivery businesses, family-tray/catering, fast food | High and stable daily deliveries | Use only when routes are dense and stable |
| Outsourced delivery | Mostly variable | Low | High | Low to medium | SLA, handoff, blame, refund disputes | Restaurants that want delivery without fleet management | Delivery orders exist but not enough for owned fleet | Best default if no own fleet is justified |
| Delivery API/provider integration | Mixed | Medium | Medium to high | Medium to high | Integration complexity and vendor dependency | POS-connected or multi-channel operators | Enough volume to justify setup | Use when it replaces enough manual work to pay back |
| Hybrid model | Mixed | Medium | Medium | High | Complexity, zone rules, operational confusion | Restaurants with nearby pickup/core zone and longer-tail delivery | Split demand by radius or urgency | Strongest if the restaurant can define clear routing rules |
| Aggregator model | Mostly variable | Low | High | Low operationally, high commercial dependence | Margin compression, data loss, weak control | Discovery-led restaurants, new brands, demand-generation channels | Existing aggregator demand | Keep as discovery engine, not sole owned-channel strategy |
| WhatsApp/manual model | Mostly variable with hidden labor | Low | Medium to high | High because of manual corrections | Errors, missed messages, ambiguous totals, weak audit trail | Very small restaurants, low ticket volume, familiar local customer base | Existing chat order volume | Use as the simplest near-term alternative; formalize only if volume rises |

## Recommended Ranking by Economic Clarity

1. Pickup only.
2. Existing staff/driver delivery.
3. Outsourced delivery.
4. Hybrid model.
5. WhatsApp/manual ordering.
6. Delivery API/provider integration.
7. Hired rider.
8. Motorcycle plus rider.
9. Motorcycle only.
10. Aggregator-only dependence for owned-channel ROI testing.
