# Chairman Verdict

## Decision Summary

The council's first ROI decision is that owned direct ordering is economically justified only for restaurants with enough repeat demand and the right fulfilment shape. Pickup-only, existing-driver delivery, outsourced delivery, and hybrid fulfilment are the only broadly defensible positive-ROI patterns in this run.

## Confirmed

- Aggregators remain discovery engines.
- Pickup-only is the lowest-burden positive-ROI starting point.
- Existing-driver delivery can be positive ROI if the driver already exists.
- Outsourced delivery is the safest bridge when owned fleet economics are weak.
- WhatsApp/manual ordering is a valid alternative but not a scalable end state.

## Proposed

- Test pickup-first on neighborhood QSR, fast casual, and family-tray/catering-led restaurants.
- Use hybrid fulfilment only when zone rules are clear.
- Use website plus WhatsApp as the default channel stack, with Telegram optional.

## Rejected

- Motorcycle-only as a first positive-ROI test.
- Any claim that owned direct ordering replaces aggregators.
- Any exact payback or margin claim without restaurant-specific inputs.

## Deferred

- Final tiers and final public prices.
- Broad feature packaging.
- POS and delivery integrations as standard inclusions.

## Unresolved

- Precise monthly order thresholds by segment.
- Actual UAE rider, bike, and outsourced-delivery cost curves.
- Provider-side support cost per account.

## Preserved Dissent

- Pickup-only may be too weak to sell in some markets.
- Hybrid may be the more practical operational answer once delivery exists.
- WhatsApp/manual ordering may beat a structured page for very small restaurants.

## Evidence Auditor Restrictions

- Do not convert scenario assumptions into facts.
- Do not claim universal profitability.
- Do not claim exact Dubai-wide thresholds.

## Evidence Gates Before Deliverables

- Collect restaurant-specific order volume, AOV, gross margin, delivery mix, radius, and fulfillment cost.
- Validate whether direct orders shift meaningful commission-heavy volume.

## What Would Change the Decision

- A restaurant segment with consistently strong direct conversion and repeat rate.
- A delivery density threshold that justifies owned fleet capacity.
- Evidence that WhatsApp/manual ordering remains lower-cost even after errors and labor are counted.
