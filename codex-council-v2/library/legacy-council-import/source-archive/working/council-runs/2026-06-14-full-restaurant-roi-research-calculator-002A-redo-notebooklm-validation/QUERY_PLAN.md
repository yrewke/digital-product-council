## Questions

### Q-001: Restaurant ROI

Validate these Run 2A-Redo calculator assumptions using only existing notebook sources: marketplace deductions of 15%/25%/35%; shifting 2%/7%/15% of total orders to direct; incremental direct orders of 0%/2%/5%; manual-order time savings of 3/5/8 minutes; mistake reduction of 1%/3%/5%; catering/group-order wins of 0/1/3 monthly; and the decision not to automatically monetize trust, SEO, AOV uplift, loyalty, or customer data. For each item, classify it as supported range, contradicted, or unsupported scenario assumption. Cite source titles and numbered citations, state geography/source type/limitations, and recommend the safest conservative/base/optimistic calculator treatment. Do not invent missing evidence.

### Q-002: Delivery And Fulfilment

Validate Run 2A-Redo's delivery-fulfilment calculator design using only existing notebook sources. Identify credible UAE ranges or bounded evidence for outsourced hot-food delivery cost per order, own-rider total monthly cost, motorcycle/fuel/insurance/maintenance costs, failed-delivery/refund risk, zone/minimum-order rules, pickup economics, and API/integration constraints. Distinguish verified facts, estimates, and vendor claims. State which values are unsafe as defaults and what owner-entered variables/formulas the calculator should use instead. Cite source titles and numbered citations.

### Q-003: Competitors And Alternatives

Validate Run 2A-Redo's competitor and alternative-cost logic using only existing notebook sources. Compare manual WhatsApp/phone ordering, Instagram/Google-only presence, QR menus, website agencies, ordering SaaS, POS ordering modules, aggregators, and doing nothing. Identify credible pricing/fee ranges, data-ownership or contract constraints, pass-through costs, and situations where alternatives are good enough. State which competitor numbers are safe or unsafe in a customer-facing ROI calculator and cite source titles and numbered citations.

### Q-004: Archetype And Provider Fit

Validate Run 2A-Redo's early-target and provider-viability conclusions using only existing notebook sources. Assess which restaurant archetypes are most likely to realize owned-channel ROI, which are poor early targets, what operational/adoption conditions are required, and what onboarding, support, customization, churn, and one-developer continuity risks could erase ROI. Separate UAE evidence from generic SaaS inference. Identify the most dangerous assumptions in the ROI calculator and recommend corrections, with source titles and numbered citations.
