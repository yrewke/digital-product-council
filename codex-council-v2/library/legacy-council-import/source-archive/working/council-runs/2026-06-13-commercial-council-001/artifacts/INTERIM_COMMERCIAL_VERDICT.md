# Chairman Interim Commercial Verdict

**Run ID:** `2026-06-13-commercial-council-001`
**Run status:** Run 1 deliberation complete
**Overall classification:** **Proposed for bounded validation; unresolved for broad sale or final deliverables**

## Decision Summary

Run 1 supports a bounded, testable commercial direction, not a finalized offer.

What we are actually selling at this stage is a **controlled validation engagement for a standardized branded owned-ordering capability**. It gives a suitable restaurant a structured direct route, menu and order intake, defined operating controls, and access to first-party customer data while the restaurant keeps its existing channels active. The engagement tests whether the channel is useful, operationally manageable, and economically credible for that restaurant. It is not yet a proven recurring software package, an aggregator-replacement product, or a demonstrated profit-improvement service.

The clearest positioning is:

> Test a branded direct-ordering route under explicit operating and commercial boundaries while continuing to use existing channels. Measure customer use, restaurant workload, operational reliability, provider effort, and net contribution before deciding whether the route should become a recurring service or gain integrations.

This positioning is **Proposed**, not Confirmed. Aggregator coexistence is an available and honest framing, but profitable repeat-demand capture remains unproven (`A-002`, `E-018`, `E-019`, `GAP-005`).

The council authorizes a **bounded Run 2 pilot portfolio** and refuses final public packages, prices, ROI claims, sales copy, or broad commercial deliverables while critical gates remain unresolved. A single favorable pilot will not be enough to confirm segment fit, repeatability, provider economics, or a preferred pricing model.

## Add-Ons and Delayed Capabilities

### Proposed Finite Add-Ons for Validation

- Additional bounded data/menu cleanup, extra onboarding or training, extra review cycles, additional locations, approved template-level personalization, and managed content changes.
- Each item must have explicit scope, acceptance criteria, responsibility, and measured provider effort.
- **Evidence strength:** Low to moderate; scoping is a defensible control, but buyer acceptance and actual labor containment are unproven (`E-021`, `E-023`, `GAP-006`).
- **What changes classification:** Repeated evidence that the work remains finite, buyers accept the boundary, and contribution remains viable across accounts.

### Deferred Capabilities

- POS and order-management integrations as standard inclusions.
- Owned or outsourced delivery as a standard inclusion.
- WhatsApp automation, advanced loyalty/promotions, QR dine-in ordering, catering execution, reservations, complex migrations, and per-order pricing.
- Material custom design, restaurant-specific workflows, and capabilities carrying unverified operational SLAs.

These capabilities may be useful, but their customer value, onboarding burden, support load, reliability exposure, and provider economics are not established (`E-006`, `E-008` through `E-017`, `E-021`, `E-023`, `GAP-005`, `GAP-006`).

## Segment Fit and Poor Fits

### Initial Recruitment Hypothesis

**Proposed:** Recruit QSR, fast-casual, and neighborhood concepts with plausible repeat demand, a reachable customer base, predictable preparation, a workable pickup point, and a manager able to own the workflow.

- **Rationale:** This is the strongest available segment inference and provides a bounded place to test (`E-004`).
- **Dissent:** No direct product evidence establishes superior conversion, willingness to pay, retention, or provider margin in these segments.
- **Evidence strength:** Low to moderate inference; not measured fit.
- **What changes classification:** Multi-account evidence showing purchase choice, usage, operational reliability, retention, net restaurant contribution, and viable provider contribution by archetype.

### Lower-Priority or Poor Initial Fits

**Proposed exclusion from initial recruitment:** restaurants with weak repeat-demand evidence, heavy dependence on aggregator discovery, unreliable menu/status discipline, highly variable preparation, no safe handoff workflow, fragmented ownership, high customization requirements, or an immediate requirement for deep POS/delivery integration.

**Deferred as target segments:** premium/fine dining and exposed mid-market casual concepts unless a distinct direct-channel use case is demonstrated.

- **Rationale:** These conditions increase the risk that doing nothing, aggregators, WhatsApp, an agency, or an established POS ecosystem is the better alternative (`E-004`, `E-015`, `E-017`, `E-018`, `E-023`).
- **Dissent:** The segment evidence is inferential and does not justify categorical exclusion.
- **Evidence strength:** Low to moderate.
- **What changes classification:** Restaurant-specific evidence of a clear job, willingness to adopt, manageable operations, and viable economics.

## Recurring Responsibilities

### Proposed Restaurant Responsibilities

- Maintain menu/content and availability.
- Assign a named operating owner and fallback for every service period.
- Accept orders, maintain statuses, communicate exceptions, and complete handoff checks.
- Keep customer-facing promises capacity-aware.
- Complete agreed onboarding inputs and use the approved fallback process.

### Proposed Provider Responsibilities

- Maintain the standardized capability and documented operating controls.
- Provide bounded onboarding, standard help guidance, and explicitly defined support/escalation.
- Provide data access/export and apply agreed incident, cancellation, migration, and exit terms.
- Measure provider effort, support demand, and recurring interventions.

### Unresolved Responsibility Gates

- Whether restaurants will reliably self-serve and sustain shift discipline.
- Whether business-hours support is acceptable and sufficient.
- Responsibility for failures, refunds, outages, data handling/export, third-party incidents, cancellation, migration, and exit.
- One-developer continuity and incident coverage during restaurant operating hours.

Self-service and bounded support are required proposed boundaries, not validated customer behaviors or proven provider economics (`E-022`, `GAP-006`).

## Pricing-Model Direction

**Proposed pricing experiment:** Test a bounded onboarding/setup component plus a recurring standardized-service component, with separately scoped finite work. Keep all amounts as hypotheses. Expose restaurant value and provider workload separately.

**Deferred:** Per-order, performance, value-based, and hybrid variable pricing until profitable channel shift and variable responsibility are measured.

**Rejected:** Low-price/high-touch positioning, opaque commission-like pricing, or any pricing rationale based on promised conversion, savings, ROI, or payback.

- **Rationale:** Setup, subscription, hybrid, and commission-free-positioned models exist, but model existence does not establish willingness to pay, recurring value, retention, or profitability (`E-024`, `E-025`, `GAP-006`).
- **Dissent:** A narrow core may not justify recurring payment; integrations and support may be necessary for value but could destroy provider repeatability.
- **Evidence strength:** Low; scenario assumption only.
- **What changes classification:** Observed willingness to pay, retention, restaurant net contribution, acquisition effort, onboarding labor, support load, churn signals, and provider contribution across multiple accounts.

No final public price, price range, discount, ROI calculator, savings estimate, or payback period is authorized.

## Confirmed

### C-01: Use a bounded pilot portfolio before broad sale or final deliverables

- **Rationale:** The packet can support a controlled validation thesis but cannot establish the smallest sellable package, restaurant payback, or provider repeatability.
- **Dissent:** The Devil's Advocate argues that a paid operational/demand-validation service using existing channels may be a better starting business than an owned-channel product. This alternative must remain in the Run 2 comparison.
- **Evidence strength:** Strong as a risk-control decision; it does not confirm market demand.
- **What changes classification:** This remains Confirmed unless a higher-quality evidence set independently resolves `GAP-005`, `GAP-006`, and the operational/contractual gates without pilots.

### C-02: Do not position the offer as aggregator replacement or general superiority

- **Rationale:** Aggregators retain discovery and convenience advantages; direct ordering may not improve customer price or experience, and profitable migration is unquantified (`E-018`, `E-019`, `GAP-005`).
- **Dissent:** Owners may value control and reduced fee exposure, but those concerns do not establish a superior net outcome.
- **Evidence strength:** Strong contradiction and unresolved-gap support.
- **What changes classification:** Comparable evidence of profitable channel shift and customer adoption could support a stronger, still bounded claim.

### C-03: Package and scope decisions must follow responsibility and operational burden, not feature count

- **Rationale:** Delivery, POS, customization, support, and managed operations create materially distinct onboarding, reliability, and cost obligations (`E-008` through `E-013`, `E-017`, `E-021` through `E-023`).
- **Dissent:** Buyers may prefer bundled simplicity and may not accept separate scopes.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate inference, sufficient as an internal design rule.
- **What changes classification:** Buyer testing showing that responsibility-based boundaries create unacceptable confusion or materially reduce viable adoption.

### C-04: Preserve all outcome, price, support, and SLA restrictions

- **Rationale:** No numeric ROI, conversion, payback, willingness-to-pay, support allowance, churn, gross margin, or final price is supported. Vendor uplift, delivery, rapid-launch, and support claims are unsafe as provider promises.
- **Dissent:** None material; advisors differ on offer breadth, not on the need to avoid unsupported promises.
- **Evidence strength:** Strong.
- **What changes classification:** Appropriately scoped primary evidence and accepted operating/contractual terms.

## Proposed

### P-01: Sell only a controlled validation engagement in Run 2

- **Rationale:** It permits testing of the owned-channel thesis without presenting it as a proven recurring product.
- **Dissent:** The neglected alternative is to validate demand and operations entirely through existing channels, or implement/partner with an established ecosystem, before offering an owned capability.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate council support; low market proof.
- **What changes classification:** Confirm if multiple restaurants choose, use, retain, and economically justify the standardized capability within bounded provider effort. Reject if existing-channel validation, WhatsApp, POS ecosystems, or doing nothing consistently win.

### P-02: Use pickup-first as the initial controlled test configuration

- **Rationale:** It removes delivery-partner dependencies and makes restaurant workflow, handoff, and peak behavior easier to observe.
- **Dissent:** Pickup-first may be commercially too weak, may under-test delivery-dependent demand, and may add a second queue. It is not confirmed as the smallest useful or sellable offer.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate operational inference; weak commercial evidence.
- **What changes classification:** Confirm only if pickup-first demonstrates buyer choice, meaningful usage, acceptable restaurant workload, peak reliability, retention, and viable provider effort. Reject or broaden if POS/delivery connection is required for usefulness.

### P-03: Test standardized self-service and bounded support as provider boundaries

- **Rationale:** Without these boundaries, a one-developer provider risks becoming an open-ended managed service or custom agency.
- **Dissent:** Restaurant discipline, self-service completion, business-hours support acceptance, and one-person continuity are unproven.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate risk logic; weak behavioral and economic proof.
- **What changes classification:** Confirm if recurring provider intervention and incident coverage remain within explicit measured allowances across accounts. Reject if restaurants require persistent managed operation or unsustainable coverage.

### P-04: Test setup-plus-recurring terms and finite add-ons

- **Rationale:** The structure exposes onboarding work, ongoing service, and exceptional work separately.
- **Dissent:** It may describe invoicing discipline without solving willingness-to-pay, recurring value, retention, or margin.
- **Evidence strength:** Low.
- **What changes classification:** Confirm only after observed willingness to pay, retention, and viable provider contribution. Replace if another model performs better under measured conditions.

## Rejected

### R-01: Broad all-in-one launch

- **Rationale:** Bundling delivery, POS, loyalty, automation, custom workflows, and broad support before value and burden are measured creates uncontrolled operational and provider risk.
- **Dissent:** Broader integration may be necessary to make the offer useful; that case belongs in gated comparison, not the base scope.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate risk evidence plus unresolved provider economics.
- **What changes classification:** Only measured evidence that a broader standardized bundle improves adoption and retention while remaining operationally and economically bounded.

### R-02: Bespoke or open-ended service obligations

- **Rationale:** Bespoke websites/features, restaurant-specific workflows, unlimited revisions, informal custom requests, 24/7 human support, dedicated success management, and open-ended managed operations conflict with the standardized one-developer premise (`E-022`, `E-023`, `GAP-006`).
- **Dissent:** Local personalization may be necessary to close or retain some accounts.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate contradiction and strong scope-risk logic.
- **What changes classification:** A separately staffed and economically validated service model, outside the current standardized-product thesis.

### R-03: Guaranteed outcomes or unsafe sales claims

- **Rationale:** Reject guarantees or broad claims about conversion, order growth, AOV, repeat orders, loyalty, savings, margin, ROI, payback, migration, labor reduction, launch speed, delivery time, reliability, seamless integration, scalability, or profitability.
- **Dissent:** None material.
- **Evidence strength:** Strong auditor restriction.
- **What changes classification:** Appropriately scoped primary evidence may permit narrow factual claims, not automatic general guarantees.

### R-04: Treating a single successful pilot as commercial validation

- **Rationale:** One configuration working for one restaurant cannot establish segment fit, repeatability, churn, provider economics, or a preferred pricing model.
- **Dissent:** A single pilot can still validate that one configuration works for that restaurant.
- **Evidence strength:** Strong methodological restriction.
- **What changes classification:** Nothing; generalization requires a portfolio.

## Evidence-Backed Conclusions

- Aggregator fees can compress restaurant margins, and some GCC operators object to lost customer data/control; magnitude and current applicability vary (`E-005`).
- Timeliness and order accuracy mattered to retention in one limited UAE study of 100 respondents aged 18-25; this cannot be generalized to all customers (`E-003`).
- Technical staging and webhook validation are possible, but do not validate restaurant operations, SLAs, adoption, or economics (`E-010`).
- Generic SaaS evidence supports caution about low-ARPA/SMB retention, not a UAE pricing or profitability conclusion (`E-024`).
- Branded direct-ordering, first-party-data access, POS connections, and delivery integrations exist as vendor-described capabilities; capability is not proof of customer value or provider viability (`E-008`, `E-014`, `E-017`).

## Unsupported or Weak Conclusions

- Pickup-first is the strongest commercial offer or the smallest sellable package.
- A standalone core is useful without POS or delivery integration.
- Aggregator coexistence will produce profitable repeat-demand capture.
- QSR, fast-casual, or neighborhood restaurants are proven best-fit segments.
- Restaurants will self-serve reliably or accept bounded business-hours support.
- A one-developer provider can repeatedly acquire, onboard, support, and retain customers profitably.
- Setup plus recurring subscription is the preferred or profitable pricing model.
- Delivery/POS deferral improves economics, or their addition creates net value.
- Any conversion, savings, margin, ROI, payback, launch-time, SLA, labor-saving, or final price conclusion.

## Contradictions to Preserve

1. **Operational safety versus commercial usefulness:** Pickup-first/non-integrated scope improves observability but may be too weak or add a second queue.
2. **Aggregator coexistence versus economic reason to buy:** Honest coexistence positioning avoids overclaiming, but profitable shift remains unproven.
3. **Standardization versus local personalization:** Boundaries protect repeatability but may reduce close rate and retention.
4. **Self-service versus restaurant discipline:** Self-service protects provider capacity but may undermine restaurant reliability.
5. **Deferring integrations versus authoritative workflow:** Deferral limits early burden but may preserve reconciliation work or remove required value.
6. **Recurring subscription versus unproven recurring value:** The model may align with ongoing service but lacks willingness-to-pay, retention, and margin evidence.
7. **Narrow scope versus provider economics:** A bounded core may be repeatable but unable to command enough revenue; broader work may recreate an agency.

## Evidence Gates Before Deliverables

Critical gates remain unresolved. Therefore, the Chairman **refuses final commercial deliverables**, including public package/pricing pages, final sales copy, ROI calculators, savings claims, and a broad go-to-market verdict.

Before deliverables, Run 2 must produce primary validation by restaurant archetype and package boundary for:

- `GAP-005`: incremental or demonstrably shifted activity, net contribution, payback inputs, and retention.
- `GAP-006`: acquisition, onboarding, support, recurring interventions, customization, churn signals, cost-to-serve, contribution, and continuity.
- Pickup/standalone-core usefulness, operational burden, peak performance, and retention.
- POS/delivery add-on burden and net value.
- Self-service completion and failure behavior.
- Accepted responsibility, refund, outage, data portability, cancellation, migration, exit, and continuity terms.

## Is Another Round Justified?

**Yes, but only as a bounded primary-validation round.** Another broad research round is not justified by Run 1. Run 2 should be a measured multi-account pilot portfolio with explicit comparison against relevant alternatives and stop/narrow rules.

Run 2 is justified because it can directly resolve the council's load-bearing gaps and disagreement. It must not be used to manufacture confidence from more vendor claims or to proceed into final deliverables before the gates are met.

## What Would Change the Overall Decision

The direction can advance from **Proposed** to **Confirmed for a bounded commercial offer** only if multiple likely-fit restaurants choose and retain a clearly bounded configuration; the configuration creates acceptable restaurant value and operations; provider effort and contribution remain viable; customization and support boundaries are accepted; and responsibility/continuity terms are workable.

The direction should be **Rejected or materially reframed** if existing-channel validation, WhatsApp, aggregators, established POS ecosystems, or doing nothing repeatedly win; pickup/standalone scope is too weak; integrations make delivery unbounded; restaurants do not self-serve; direct activity is not economically meaningful; or provider acquisition, onboarding, support, and continuity cannot be contained.
