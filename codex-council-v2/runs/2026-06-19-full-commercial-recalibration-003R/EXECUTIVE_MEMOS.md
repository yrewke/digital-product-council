## Position Summary

Contrarian: constrain the model; do not sell marketplace replacement, fast payback, or uncapped support. Use low migration assumptions until pilots prove otherwise.

First-Principles: model direct ordering by causal variables and channel flows. Separate shifted, incremental, pickup, delivery, manual-order structuring, and catering.

Expansionist: the product is larger than commission saving. Owned customer data, repeat activation, catering, pickup, analytics, and credibility matter, but not all are immediate cash ROI.

Outsider: alternatives are legitimate. Sell only where the product beats the buyer's next-best alternative, and recommend hygiene-only or smaller starts when appropriate.

Executor: use a staged hybrid product model with explicit setup, monthly, support, pass-through, module, custom quote, and pilot boundaries.
