# Devil's Advocate Attack

The commercial model can still fail if sales turns broad strategic value into implied payback, assumes customers will switch from marketplaces without a reason, hides delivery failure under software language, or allows modules to become uncontrolled custom work.

Weakest assumptions:

- Marketplace-to-direct migration rate.
- Customer incentive cost.
- Delivery cost and SLA reliability.
- Restaurant willingness to pay before proof.
- Provider support burden.
- Integration and AI usage creep.

Defeating changes adopted:

- ROI is diagnostic, not a promise.
- Conservative/base/optimistic scenarios are required.
- Delivery is a hard gate.
- Support caps and pass-throughs are explicit.
- POS/delivery integrations are custom quote.
- Pilot data replaces planning assumptions.
