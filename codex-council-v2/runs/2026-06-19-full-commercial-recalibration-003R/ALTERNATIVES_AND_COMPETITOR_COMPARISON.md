# Alternatives and Competitor Comparison

| Alternative | Best Fit | Weakness vs Direct-Ordering Product | Sales Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Do nothing | Marketplace-dependent restaurant with no direct demand or cash | No owned asset, no data, no direct repeat mechanism | Accept if value/readiness are low. |
| Marketplace only | Discovery-led kitchens and low brand pull | Commission burden, no first-party customer relationship, platform dependence | Do not promise replacement; test direct for repeat customers only. |
| Google Business Profile + Instagram | Discovery and hygiene | Not structured ordering or CRM by itself | Recommend before heavy build if basics are missing. |
| Phone/WhatsApp manual | Low volume, relationship-led orders | Errors, lost records, staff back-and-forth, no automation | Upgrade when volume/friction appears. |
| Wix/WordPress/freelancer | Static site and credibility | Variable quality/support; not automatically ordering, CRM, delivery, analytics | Compete only when operational direct ordering matters. |
| Ordering SaaS | Standardized online ordering | Less bespoke; may not fit local workflows/integrations | Use as market ceiling signal. |
| POS ecosystem | Mature operators needing operational backbone | Bigger adoption burden; may be overkill for small restaurants | Do not compete head-on when POS backbone is primary need. |
| Agency custom build | Brand-heavy custom site | Higher cost; support and restaurant ops may be weak | Compete on restaurant-specific ROI and operations. |
| Internal staff | Multi-branch operator with tech/ops capacity | Hiring/management burden | Sell advisory/custom only if internal team exists. |

Current competitor anchors inspected: Foodics, Sapaad, Wix, GloriaFood, Owner.com, Careem Express, Deliverect/Careem Express.
