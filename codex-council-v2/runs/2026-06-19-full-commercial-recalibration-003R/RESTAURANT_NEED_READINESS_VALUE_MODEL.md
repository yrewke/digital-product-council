# Restaurant Need, Readiness, and Value Model

Do not infer low need from missing assets. A restaurant with no database, no owned channel, no structured ordering, or no direct relationship may have high need but low readiness.

| Dimension | What To Assess | High Signal | Commercial Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Need | Marketplace dependence, high commission burden, no customer data, weak direct channel, manual order errors, poor repeat mechanism, no catering flow. | Owner feels pain and can show order history or operational friction. | Build diagnostic and stage the smallest useful product. |
| Potential value | Eligible repeat orders, AOV, margin, pickup potential, catering/group demand, direct prompt reach, brand search volume. | Existing demand with reachable repeat customers. | Model ROI by channel and start with highest-confidence value path. |
| Readiness | Menu cleanliness, staff discipline, fulfillment capacity, owner availability, photos/content, payment preference, branch complexity. | Can implement prompts, update menu, and handle orders reliably. | Proceed with package; otherwise do prep sprint or smaller entry product. |
| Starting scope | Smallest product that creates a measurable behavior change. | Clear next customer action. | Entry presence, ordering page, website+ordering, dashboard, or commerce system. |

## Qualification Rule

High need + low readiness = staged rollout, not rejection. Low need + high readiness = do not oversell. High value + high readiness = best pilot candidate.
