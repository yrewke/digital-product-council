# Devil's Advocate

Attack: Devil's Advocate attack: the model can still over-sell by turning broad strategic value into implied payback, underestimating direct-customer switching friction, hiding delivery failure under channel migration, and letting modules expand into unsupported custom work. The weakest points are migration rate, incentive cost, delivery economics, support load, and willingness to pay before proof.
Defeating change: Defeat the attack by labeling every number, using conservative/base/optimistic scenarios, putting pass-throughs/support caps/exclusions on the offer, gating delivery and POS integrations, making ROI diagnostic not promised, and replacing assumptions with pilot measurements.
