## Core Evidence Findings

1. Marketplace migration is not automatic. Customers stay with marketplaces for convenience, stored payments, discounts, subscriptions, tracking, refund trust, and discovery.
2. Commission saving is not profit. Shifted-order value equals avoided marketplace burden minus direct payment, delivery, incentive, loyalty, marketing, refund, and operational costs.
3. Delivery can erase the margin case. Direct ordering without physical fulfillment capacity can create a worse customer experience and poor unit economics.
4. The product can still create real value through structured ordering, first-party data, repeat activation, pickup, catering capture, menu clarity, analytics, and reduced manual handling.
5. Strategic value must not be booked as immediate AED ROI unless there is an activation mechanism and measured conversion.
6. Provider economics require setup fees, recurring support, caps, pass-throughs, and custom-quote boundaries. Unlimited support at low subscription prices is commercially unsafe.
