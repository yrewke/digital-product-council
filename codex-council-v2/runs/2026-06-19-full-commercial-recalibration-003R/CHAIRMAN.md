# Chairman File

## First Synthesis
Chairman first synthesis: adopt a staged hybrid commercial model. Target repeat-demand restaurants with measurable direct-order potential first, but separate need, value, and readiness. Retain marketplaces as discovery and overflow channels; do not promise replacement. ROI must separate incremental orders, shifted marketplace orders, structured phone/WhatsApp orders, pickup, delivery, and catering. Product should use buyable tiers plus optional modules and custom-quote integrations. Pricing must triangulate value, affordability, alternatives, support burden, provider cost, and pilot risk. Delivery capacity is a hard gate, not a software promise.

## Devil's Advocate Considered
Devil's Advocate attack: the model can still over-sell by turning broad strategic value into implied payback, underestimating direct-customer switching friction, hiding delivery failure under channel migration, and letting modules expand into unsupported custom work. The weakest points are migration rate, incentive cost, delivery economics, support load, and willingness to pay before proof.

## Provisional Verdict
Provisional verdict: replace the old tier-first model with a staged hybrid model: Entry Direct Presence, Ordering Page, Website plus Ordering, Direct Orders Dashboard, and Direct Commerce, with optional WhatsApp, Telegram, CRM/loyalty, analytics, catering, delivery dispatch, AI, POS, and delivery integrations. Use internal launch prices only, not public guaranteed pricing. Early pilots should focus on repeat-demand, medium/high AOV, high marketplace dependence, owner-led restaurants where direct prompts and fulfillment discipline are realistic.

## Final Verdict
Final commercial recalibration: use a staged hybrid model with buyable core products, optional modules, custom-quote integrations, restaurant-specific ROI diagnostics, and delivery feasibility gates. The model must include number labels, support caps, pass-throughs, and pilot validation. Do not claim marketplace replacement, guaranteed ROI, universal POS integration, or delivery capacity the restaurant does not physically have. Target first pilots at repeat-demand restaurants with medium/high AOV, visible existing demand, marketplace dependence, and owner ability to prompt customers. Treat lack of readiness as a staging issue, not absence of need.
