# Chairman Final ROI Verdict

## Decision Summary

Proceed with a two-mode ROI calculator:

1. A **public quick diagnostic** that identifies likely ROI paths, data gaps, risks, best alternatives, and the next measurement or test.
2. An **evidence-based internal/sales model** that calculates AED impact and payback only after restaurant-specific records and all material costs are entered.

The strongest ROI story is not “a website saves commission.” It is:

> Use the smallest owned-channel intervention to solve one measured restaurant problem, then calculate net value from actual shifted demand, incremental demand, operational leakage, fulfilment economics, or high-ticket lead capture.

## Confirmed

- Aggregators remain discovery engines; do not claim replacement.
- Shifted orders and incremental orders must remain separate.
- Avoided deductions are not net profit.
- Staff capacity released is not automatically cash saved.
- Trust, SEO, credibility, customer data, and strategic optionality are not automatically monetized.
- Positive causal defaults must be zero/blank or explicit opt-in.
- Best-alternative and doing-nothing comparisons are mandatory.
- The calculator may recommend “do not buy yet.”

## Proposed

- Recruit pilots from WhatsApp-heavy, family-tray/catering-led, aggregator-heavy, pickup-capable, and weak-website-with-demand restaurants that possess usable records and a named operator.
- Test website/order page plus familiar handoff/notification as a small reversible stack where it matches the measured problem.
- Test pickup or existing fulfilment before new fleet or complex integration.
- Treat bots, AI, POS, and delivery APIs as separately gated modules.

## Rejected

- Public benchmark-style likely ROI predictions.
- Guaranteed savings, growth, payback, conversion, AOV uplift, time savings, or error reduction.
- Universal archetype, channel, or fulfilment recommendations.
- Using parcel/ecommerce delivery prices as restaurant delivery defaults.
- Using advertised competitor prices as equivalent-scope pricing evidence.
- Monetizing customer data without measured activation and outcomes.

## Strongest ROI Paths

1. Actual-statement-based net benefit from repeat orders shifted direct.
2. Known delivery-zone, fee, or pickup-cost leakage correction.
3. Measured WhatsApp/phone missed-order, error, and handling leakage.
4. Tracked incremental catering/family-tray/group-order contribution.
5. Source-tracked incremental direct orders.

## Weakest ROI Paths

1. Generic trust or website credibility converted to AED.
2. Generic SEO or Google uplift.
3. Vendor-claimed AOV or loyalty uplift.
4. AI/bot conversion before base demand and workflow are proven.
5. Customer-data value without measured repeat activation.

## Calculator Minimum Gate for AED/Payback

Require monthly orders, AOV, defined contribution margin, actual marketplace deductions if used, direct payment/promotion/fulfilment costs, setup and recurring costs, and explicit classification of shifted versus incremental orders. Require additional records for operational, delivery, or lead modules.

## “This Works Only If”

The restaurant has reachable demand, assigns an operational owner, maintains accurate menu/availability, fulfils reliably, promotes the relevant direct path, and produces enough measured net value after every cost.

## “Do Not Buy Yet If”

Demand is absent, records are unavailable, staff cannot own the queue, fulfilment economics are unknown, conservative net impact is non-positive, or an existing POS/SaaS/manual process solves the job better.

## What Run 2B May Use

Run 2B may package around measured ROI jobs and responsibility boundaries. It may use the strongest ROI-backed core/add-on/delay classifications in the handoff. It may not finalize public prices from these numbers alone.
