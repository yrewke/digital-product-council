# Devil's Advocate Attack

**Emerging direction attacked:** Positive ROI is most likely with pickup, existing-driver delivery, outsourced delivery, or hybrid fulfilment.
**Strongest counter-case:** For many UAE restaurants, direct ordering never creates enough incremental demand to pay for setup, support, and fulfilment complexity.
**Neglected alternative:** Stay on aggregators and WhatsApp/manual ordering, and do nothing more until the restaurant proves repeat demand.
**Weakest ROI assumption:** That repeat orders can be converted at a high enough rate to beat simple manual workflows.
**Hidden support or operational debt:** Every extra channel adds menu maintenance, status discipline, support load, and refund risk.
**Failure scenario:** The restaurant buys software, then keeps taking orders in chat, while the owner still relies on aggregators for discovery and never sees enough direct volume.
**Evidence that would defeat this attack:** Multiple restaurants showing that direct orders shift meaningful commission-heavy volume into a structured channel with measurable retained margin.
