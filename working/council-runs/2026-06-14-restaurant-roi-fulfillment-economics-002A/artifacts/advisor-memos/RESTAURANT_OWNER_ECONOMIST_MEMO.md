# Advisor Memo: Restaurant Owner Economist

**Commercial question:** Under which fulfilment model does owned direct ordering create positive ROI versus alternatives?
**Verdict:** conditional
**Strongest finding:** Pickup-only and existing-driver delivery have the best chance of positive ROI because they avoid the highest variable cost and reduce dependency on a new fleet.
**Evidence used:** Run 001 evidence on aggregator margin pressure, customer-data/control loss, and direct-channel capability; Run 002A scenario assumptions on fixed-cost dilution.
**Critical assumption:** The restaurant already has some repeat demand or nearby traffic that can be captured.
**Tradeoff:** The more delivery control you want, the more fixed cost you introduce.
**Disagreement expected:** The operator will push the case that pickup-only is too weak to sell; I agree it is weak commercially but still the cleanest economic test.
**Evidence request:** Segment-specific monthly order volumes and gross margin by restaurant archetype.
