# Independent Executive Memos

## Restaurant Owner Buyer

**Preferred structure:** Hybrid responsibility tiers plus controlled add-ons.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0, only with existing demand and a named monitoring owner.  
**Core:** Menu/cart, required details, stable URL, source tags, receiver, basic record.  
**Add-ons:** WhatsApp handoff, catering enquiry, QR routing, languages.  
**Delayed/custom:** Payment, bots, integrations, AI, multi-branch.  
**Refuse:** Universal integrations, unlimited support/customization, guarantees.  
**Hidden risk:** Buyer thinks order capture means reliable order management.  
**Buy trigger:** Live order demo, explicit responsibility, total-cost disclosure.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Higher-responsibility expectations without support caps.

## Restaurant Operator

**Preferred structure:** Hybrid responsibility tiers with strict operational gates.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 for low-volume disciplined operators; Tier 2 is the smallest operationally credible package for meaningful direct volume.  
**Core:** Tier 2 queue, auth, accept/reject/status, availability, pickup/manual delivery details.  
**Add-ons:** Enquiries, notifications, payment, zones, table QR after fit.  
**Delayed/custom:** Kitchen, multi-branch, advanced reporting, integrations, bots, AI.  
**Refuse:** Provider ownership of food, fulfilment, complaints, refunds, SLA/ROI guarantees.  
**Hidden risk:** Unmonitored second queue at peak.  
**Buy trigger:** Named shift owner and measurable leakage.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Staff fail statuses/availability and expect rescue.

## ROI Translator

**Preferred structure:** Hybrid ladder mapped to one measured job per sale.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 where order capture itself is the measured job; Tier 1 where destination/enquiry is the measured job; Tier 2 where operational leakage is measured.  
**Core:** Only features directly necessary to the package job and source/outcome measurement.  
**Add-ons:** Catering, WhatsApp handoff, zones, payment, bots only with specific ROI/data gate.  
**Delayed/custom:** Generic analytics, AI, integrations, advanced operations.  
**Refuse:** Upgrades justified by generic trust, SEO, AOV, loyalty, or customer-data claims.  
**Hidden risk:** Package ladder encourages feature upsell instead of job fit.  
**Buy trigger:** Dominant ROI path and conservative owner-data case.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Weak ROI job creates churn/support pressure.

## Packaging Strategist

**Preferred structure:** Three base packages plus finite modules; no fixed Tier 3 bundle. Attach standardized validation to uncertain early sales.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 as bounded decision, not proven market fact.  
**Core:** Responsibility-defining features only.  
**Add-ons:** Repeatable modules with dependency rules.  
**Delayed/custom:** High-variance commerce, integration, operations.  
**Refuse:** Pure feature pricing and broad all-in-one tiers.  
**Hidden risk:** Too many modules recreate feature-menu confusion.  
**Buy trigger:** One-sentence job distinction between packages.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Exceptions and combinations exceed catalog rules.

## Competitor and Sales Practicality Analyst

**Preferred structure:** Hybrid, but keep visible sales choices small.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 only when it beats WhatsApp/doing nothing for an observed problem.  
**Core:** Structured capture, stable link, bounded notification, record, source tags.  
**Add-ons:** WhatsApp, enquiry, QR, notifications, selected commerce/integration after qualification.  
**Delayed/custom:** Commerce combinations, AI, integrations, multi-branch, table operations.  
**Refuse:** Superiority claims, aggregator replacement, guaranteed results.  
**Hidden risk:** Additional queue and agency-like quoting.  
**Buy trigger:** Measured problem, demand, named owner.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Buyer expects marketing, bespoke design, unsupported integration, or after-hours operations.

## Channel Architect

**Preferred structure:** Base package owns the website/order backend; channel doors are modules.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 with Telegram receiver as staff channel, not proof of Telegram customer demand.  
**Core:** Deterministic menu/cart/order record.  
**Add-ons:** WhatsApp 1, Telegram 2, WhatsApp 2 after volume; notifications.  
**Delayed/custom:** Channel 3 AI assistants.  
**Refuse:** AI confirmation of price, availability, payment, delivery promise, or final order.  
**Hidden risk:** Disconnected channel-specific workflows.  
**Buy trigger:** Existing channel volume and customer behavior.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Policy changes, escalations, and unclear human handoff.

## POS and Integration Gatekeeper

**Preferred structure:** No standard integration tier; supported adapters and custom quotes only.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Must work without POS/delivery integration.  
**Core:** Manual POS entry remains restaurant responsibility through Tier 2.  
**Add-ons:** Only named supported adapter after access/test/acceptance/failure policy.  
**Delayed/custom:** Unknown or variable vendor connections.  
**Refuse:** “Any POS” or “any delivery provider.”  
**Hidden risk:** Vendor dependency becomes provider obligation.  
**Buy trigger:** Measured duplicate-entry/dispatch burden exceeds integration cost.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Closed API, vendor change, or urgent production incident.

## Delivery and Fulfilment Gatekeeper

**Preferred structure:** Fulfilment fields/workflow in Tier 2; economics-dependent rules/modules after Tier 2.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 only when restaurant already has a safe manual process.  
**Core:** Explicit pickup/manual-delivery details where relevant.  
**Add-ons:** Zones, fees, minimums, supported provider integration.  
**Delayed/custom:** Owned-driver dispatch, multiple providers, complex tracking.  
**Refuse:** Delivery SLA or provider responsibility for restaurant fulfilment.  
**Hidden risk:** Direct delivery destroys margin and creates complaints.  
**Buy trigger:** Known fulfilment model and costs.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Unknown zones/costs and expected incident rescue.

## Developer Cost and Support Analyst

**Preferred structure:** Hybrid with strict scope, allowances, recurring support, pass-through, and exceptional-work separation.  
**Smallest sellable package:** Tier 0 with named owner and fixed data allowance.  
**Core:** Bounded product support and responsibility sign-off.  
**Add-ons:** Only repeatable modules with dependencies and support allowances.  
**Delayed/custom:** AI, integrations, multi-branch, advanced operations, high-variance commerce.  
**Refuse:** Unlimited support/customization, ERP, guarantees, implied operations.  
**Hidden risk:** Restaurant/third-party failures become support obligations.  
**Buy trigger:** Accepted self-service and pass-through costs.  
**Provider-regret trigger:** Account exceeds allowances without paid re-scope.

## Evidence Auditor

**Preferred correction:** Choose decisively, but label sellability, supportability, archetype fit, and cost scores as bounded decisions/hypotheses rather than evidence.  
**Key correction:** Only Tier 0 technical scope is locked. Tier 1/2 and module classifications are decisions. Fixed Tier 3 should be split.  
**Refuse:** Unsupported “best fit,” “easy to sell,” “reduce leakage,” or “supported integration” language without gates.  
**Hidden risk:** Missing data, backup, export, privacy, incident, cancellation, and continuity obligations.  
**Required gate:** Exact allowances, exclusions, acceptance criteria, responsibilities, and provider measurements before pricing.

## Devil's Advocate

**Attack posture:** Tier 0 may be too weak, Tier 1 may be a generic website, Tier 2 may create a second queue, and modules may recreate bespoke work. A three-package story can still be too complicated if every sale needs qualification. Validation may sound unfinished. Competitors or doing nothing may win whenever demand, operator discipline, or provider continuity is weak.
