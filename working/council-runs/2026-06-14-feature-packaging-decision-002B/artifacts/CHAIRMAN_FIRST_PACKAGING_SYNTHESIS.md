# Chairman First Packaging Synthesis

## Recommended Packaging Structure

Choose a **hybrid responsibility-based structure with three base packages and a finite gated module catalog**:

1. Tier 0 - Ordering Page: order capture.
2. Tier 1 - Website + Ordering: clear owned destination plus capture.
3. Tier 2 - Direct Order Workflow: accountable staff queue and workflow.

Do not create a fixed Tier 3 bundle. Payment, delivery rules, customer records, promotions, deeper analytics, bots, and supported integrations become separately gated modules after Tier 2 or where dependencies permit.

## Smallest Sellable Package

Tier 0 is chosen as the smallest sellable package **as a bounded packaging decision**, not as proven market evidence. Sell only where existing demand and a named monitoring owner exist. Tier 2 is the smallest operationally credible package for restaurants seeking material leakage reduction or meaningful direct volume.

## Core Packages and Modules

- Tier 0 core: locked technical scope plus explicit responsibility/acceptance boundary.
- Tier 1 core: template-based presence, contact/hours/location/social/QR, and ordering.
- Tier 2 core: dashboard/auth, accept/reject/status, availability, order history, and known pickup/manual-delivery workflow.
- Modules: WhatsApp handoff/bot, Telegram customer bot, catering enquiry, languages/content, notifications, payment, delivery rules, table QR, controlled promotions, deeper analytics, and supported integrations.

## Delayed / Custom / Refused

Delay or custom-quote AI assistants, POS/delivery integrations, kitchen view, multi-branch, advanced reporting, webhooks, and complex operations. Refuse universal integrations, ERP scope, aggregator replacement, guarantees, unlimited support/customization, and implied restaurant operations.

## Support and Third-Party Policy

Every sale separates setup, recurring support, pass-through costs, and exceptional work. Every package/module states allowances, dependencies, acceptance, support limits, data/backup/export/exit, incident fallback, and restaurant responsibilities.

## Demo and Sales Implications

Build and sell Tier 0 first as the smallest real product. Build a Tier 2 workflow demo next because it provides the clearest differentiated operational story. Demonstrate Tier 1 only for an observed presence/destination job. Do not demo or quote high-risk modules before qualification.

## Questions for Final Response

- Should Tier 1 remain a base package or become a website module?
- Is Tier 0’s buyer value strong enough without WhatsApp handoff?
- Which modules may attach before Tier 2?
- How strict must launch-validation and support allowances be?
