# Role-Specific Starting Briefs

**Decision question:** Which smallest standardized package solves one measured high-value restaurant job while keeping restaurant operations, third-party costs, and one-developer support responsibility explicit and economically bounded?

## 1. Restaurant Owner Buyer
Judge whether each package is understandable, believable, affordable in structure, and worth buying now. Reject feature lists that do not explain the job, responsibility, and outcome.

## 2. Restaurant Operator
Trace staff ownership, peak handling, menu accuracy, acknowledgement, fulfilment, complaints, and refunds. Identify where a package adds a second queue or hidden work.

## 3. ROI Translator
Map every included feature to the strongest Run 2A-Redo ROI paths. Reject upgrades justified mainly by generic trust, SEO, AOV, loyalty, or novelty.

## 4. Packaging Strategist
Compare fixed tiers, pure feature pricing, hybrid tiers/add-ons, and validation-first. Optimize for sales clarity, flexibility, urgent cash, and long-term productization.

## 5. Competitor and Alternatives Analyst
Compare every package against manual WhatsApp, QR menu, agency website, ordering SaaS, POS module, aggregators, and doing nothing.

## 6. Channel Architect
Classify website, WhatsApp handoff/bot/AI, Telegram receiver/bot/AI, and hybrid stacks. Keep deterministic transaction ownership explicit.

## 7. POS and Integration Gatekeeper
Classify integrations as supported, conditional, custom, delayed, refused, or unknown. Refuse universal promises.

## 8. Delivery and Fulfilment Gatekeeper
Decide which pickup/manual delivery/zone/provider features belong now, later, custom, or never. Protect against hidden fulfilment responsibility.

## 9. Developer Cost and Support Analyst
Estimate setup, support, maintenance, third-party failures, continuity, and customization exposure. Define provider gates.

## 10. Sales Practicality Analyst
Test whether the structure can be explained in one meeting, demonstrated, and sold this week without overclaiming.

## 11. Evidence Auditor
Flag unsupported package confidence, hidden obligations, unsafe promises, scope creep, and assumptions treated as facts.

## 12. Devil's Advocate
Attack weakness, complexity, feature confusion, tier traps, integration/support hell, validation credibility, and competitor alternatives.

## 13. Chairman
Choose one packaging model, preserve dissent, specify the ladder/modules/gates, and prepare the exact Run 2C pricing handoff.

## Required Memo Ending

Each memo must decide: preferred structure; smallest sellable package; core; add-ons; delayed/custom; refused now; biggest hidden risk; owner buy trigger; provider-regret trigger.
