# Devil's Advocate Packaging Attack

## Why the Package May Be Too Weak

Tier 0 still asks the restaurant to monitor Telegram, confirm, collect payment, fulfil, and resolve complaints. Manual WhatsApp may be good enough. Tier 1 may be merely a template website. Tier 2 may still require manual POS entry and become another queue.

## Why It May Be Too Complicated

Three packages plus channel, enquiry, payment, fulfilment, QR, analytics, and integration modules can recreate feature pricing. Owners may not understand responsibility distinctions and may ask only, “Does it take orders, payments, and delivery?”

## Why Fixed Tiers and Feature Pricing Both Fail

Fixed tiers trap irrelevant features and support expectations. Feature pricing forces the owner to design the system and creates configuration variance. The hybrid model inherits both risks unless the visible catalog stays very small.

## Why Bots, POS, and Delivery Create Support Hell

They introduce vendor policies, account setup, webhooks, message templates, access approvals, outages, exceptions, and urgent restaurant-hour incidents. A one-developer provider becomes the blamed coordinator even when the third party fails.

## Why Validation Can Sound Unreal

A “launch-validation plan” can sound like the seller does not trust the product. It can also become unpaid analysis, custom reporting, and indefinite optimization.

## Why One Developer May Not Survive

Restaurant operating hours, staff turnover, menu changes, missed orders, refunds, third-party outages, and custom requests create simultaneous urgent obligations. Setup revenue does not cover permanent operational dependence.

## Why Competitors or Doing Nothing Win

WhatsApp is familiar; QR menus are cheap; agencies customize; ordering SaaS/POS modules provide mature workflows and integrations; aggregators deliver discovery and fulfilment. Doing nothing wins when demand or discipline is weak.

## Attack-Resistant Correction

Sell only a small package against a measured job, make every responsibility explicit, qualify out weak operators, keep high-risk modules hidden until qualification, and stop the relationship when support/operation gates fail.
