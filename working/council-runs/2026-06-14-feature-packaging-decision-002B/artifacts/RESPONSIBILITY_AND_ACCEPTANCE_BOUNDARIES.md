# Responsibility and Acceptance Boundaries

## Every Package Must Specify

- Exact included menu/content allowance and accepted input format.
- Included revision and training allowance.
- Restaurant dependencies and named approver/operator.
- Acceptance test and launch sign-off.
- Standard support hours/allowance, response boundary, and exclusions.
- Domain, hosting, renewal, DNS, certificate, and migration ownership.
- Data retention, backup, export, deletion, cancellation, and exit process.
- Incident handling, fallback, and third-party failure boundary.
- Competitor/doing-nothing comparison and package-specific data gate.

## Tier 0 Critical Boundary

The provider supplies order capture, Telegram delivery, and local history as defined. The restaurant owns monitoring, acknowledgement, confirmation, payment, fulfilment, food quality, customer communication, complaints, refunds, and missed-order fallback. SQLite backup/export/device-failure handling must be stated before sale.

## Tier 1 Critical Boundary

The provider supplies a template-based presence within a fixed content allowance. The restaurant supplies and approves accurate content. Custom design, photography, translation, managed content, profile management, QR printing, and ongoing content updates are separate.

## Tier 2 Critical Boundary

The provider supplies the configured order workflow and bounded training. The restaurant owns shift assignment, status discipline, menu availability, fulfilment, complaints, refunds, and manual POS entry. Staff turnover and repeated retraining are separate.

## Commerce / Integration Module Critical Boundary

Each module requires written responsibility for merchant/vendor account, fees, policies, refunds, disputes, outages, privacy, security, reconciliation, escalation, and ongoing maintenance. No adapter is “supported” until access, test, acceptance, and failure policy exist.
