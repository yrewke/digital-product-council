# Evidence Auditor Packaging Review

## Overall Disposition

The final direction may be chosen as a bounded council decision. It must not be represented as proven buyer preference, provider profitability, or archetype fit.

## Required Restrictions

- Only Tier 0 technical scope is locked. Tier 0 sellability is chosen but unvalidated.
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 boundaries are proposed product decisions.
- There is no fixed Tier 3; commerce capabilities are modules with separate gates.
- Provider cost scores are preliminary ordinal estimates, not pricing evidence.
- Archetype fit and “sellable this week” require qualification and remain hypotheses.
- No package may claim achieved leakage reduction, visibility, conversion, ROI, or reliability.

## Hidden Obligations That Must Be Defined

- SQLite backup, retention, export, device failure, deletion, and cancellation.
- Telegram missed-message fallback, monitoring, outage, and escalation.
- Domain/hosting/renewal/DNS/certificate/migration.
- Content allowances, revisions, translation, managed updates, and profile access.
- Training, staff turnover, peak incidents, security, and second-queue burden.
- Payment reconciliation, refunds, chargebacks, fraud, and gateway outages.
- Customer-record consent, access, retention, deletion, and export.
- Integration acceptance, maintenance, vendor change, and failure policy.
- Validation duration, deliverables, success criteria, and analysis allowance.

## Package Claim Policy

Call packages “designed to” solve a measured job. Use restaurant data and pilot evidence before claiming they do solve it. Require best-alternative/doing-nothing comparison before recommendation.

## Pricing/Pilot Boundary

Run 2C may price scope and obligations, but cannot convert preliminary cost scores or ROI hypotheses into final public prices without explicit assumptions and primary validation.
