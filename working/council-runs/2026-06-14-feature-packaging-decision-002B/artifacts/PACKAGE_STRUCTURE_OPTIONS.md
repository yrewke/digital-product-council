# Package Structure Options

## Option A - Fixed Tiers

**Customer sees:** A simple ascending ladder from ordering page to website, workflow, and commerce.

**Included:** Predetermined tier features.  
**Add-on:** Minimal or none.  
**Custom:** Integrations and enterprise work.  
**Refused:** Universal integration, guarantees, open-ended support.

**Pros:** Easiest to explain and demo; clear upgrade path; supports urgent cash.  
**Cons:** Forces irrelevant features; creates pressure to “include one more thing”; tier boundaries may trap provider effort.  
**Support risk:** Medium-high because buyers assume everything in the tier is supported equally.  
**Sales clarity:** Very high.  
**Developer feasibility:** Medium.  
**ROI fit:** Medium; jobs vary by archetype.  
**Urgent cash:** High.  
**Long-term productization:** Medium.

## Option B - Pure Feature Pricing

**Customer sees:** A menu of individually selected capabilities.

**Included:** Only chosen features.  
**Add-on:** Everything.  
**Custom:** Variable integrations.  
**Refused:** Unsafe promises.

**Pros:** Maximum flexibility; maps precisely to measured jobs.  
**Cons:** Owners must design their own solution; quoting and dependency logic become confusing; combinations recreate custom work.  
**Support risk:** High due to configuration variance.  
**Sales clarity:** Low.  
**Developer feasibility:** Low-medium.  
**ROI fit:** High in theory, low in practical selling.  
**Urgent cash:** Low-medium.  
**Long-term productization:** Low unless module dependencies mature.

## Option C - Hybrid Responsibility Tiers Plus Add-ons

**Customer sees:** A small fixed ladder defined by responsibility, plus optional modules for channel or measured ROI jobs.

**Included:** Tier-defining workflow and responsibility.  
**Add-on:** WhatsApp handoff/bots, Telegram customer bot, catering enquiry, payment, zones, table QR, supported integrations.  
**Custom:** Complex integrations, multi-branch, advanced operations.  
**Refused:** Universal promises, unlimited custom/support, guarantees.

**Pros:** Clear enough to buy; flexible enough for restaurant jobs; separates support burden; supports productization.  
**Cons:** Requires dependency rules and disciplined quoting; too many add-ons can still confuse.  
**Support risk:** Medium if catalog and gates are strict.  
**Sales clarity:** High.  
**Developer feasibility:** High relative to alternatives.  
**ROI fit:** High.  
**Urgent cash:** High.  
**Long-term productization:** High.

## Option D - Validation Package First, Then Modules

**Customer sees:** A bounded test engagement before selecting a product/module configuration.

**Included:** Discovery, setup of a small test, measurement, and recommendation.  
**Add-on:** Product modules after test.  
**Custom:** Complex work after evidence.  
**Refused:** Guarantees and premature integration.

**Pros:** Honest under uncertainty; collects owner/provider data; reduces wrong-fit sales.  
**Cons:** May sound like consulting or unfinished software; can delay cash/product adoption; scope can become analysis-heavy.  
**Support risk:** Medium; validation labor can become open-ended.  
**Sales clarity:** Medium-low.  
**Developer feasibility:** Medium.  
**ROI fit:** Very high.  
**Urgent cash:** Medium.  
**Long-term productization:** Medium unless standardized.

## Comparative Verdict

| Criterion | A Fixed | B Pure features | C Hybrid | D Validation-first |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Owner clarity | High | Low | High | Medium |
| ROI-job fit | Medium | High | High | High |
| Support containment | Medium | Low | High | Medium |
| Urgent cash fit | High | Medium-low | High | Medium |
| Productization | Medium | Low | High | Medium |
| Handles uncertainty | Low-medium | Medium | High with gates | Very high |

**Preliminary hypothesis for deliberation:** Option C may best balance clarity and bounded flexibility. Option D remains a live alternative or attached launch motion. Scores above are council hypotheses, not observed buyer/provider facts.
