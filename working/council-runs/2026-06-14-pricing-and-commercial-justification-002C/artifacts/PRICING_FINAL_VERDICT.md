# Pricing Final Verdict

**Status:** Final internal pricing hypothesis. Public pricing remains unresolved.
**Currency:** AED, excluding VAT, third-party costs, and exceptional work.
**Restriction:** Every numeric builder price and allowance is an internal scenario assumption.

## Executive Summary

Use three responsibility-based base packages plus gated modules. Separate setup, recurring responsibility, third-party pass-through costs, and exceptional work. Discount launch setup only. Require an alternative-fit check before quoting. Require paid discovery before every first-cohort T2 implementation.

## Chosen Pricing Structure

| Package | Launch setup | Standard setup | Provider-managed monthly | Client-owned support |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| T0 Ordering Page | 2,900 | 3,600 | 350 mandatory while managed | 250 optional |
| T1 Website + Ordering | 5,900 | 7,500 | 650 mandatory while managed | 450 optional |
| T2 Direct Order Workflow | 12,000 after discovery | 15,000 after discovery | 1,100 mandatory | Unavailable during initial cohort |

T2 discovery is an internal AED 2,500 hypothesis, paid separately and not automatically credited.

## Why This Structure

- Setup funds finite acquisition, onboarding, data, configuration, training, and acceptance.
- Recurring fees fund retained product/hosting responsibility, not restaurant operations.
- Pass-through separation prevents hidden vendor-cost promises.
- Exceptional-work pricing protects standard packages from becoming unlimited service.

## Hosting And Responsibility

| Mode | Internal hypothesis | Rule |
|---|---:|---|
| Provider-managed | Included setup; managed monthly mandatory | Launch default |
| Client-owned T0 | +750 setup; optional 250 support | Proposed |
| Client-owned T1 | +1,000 setup; optional 450 support | Proposed |
| Client-owned T2 | Not offered in initial cohort | Confirmed restriction |
| Later client-owned T2 | +1,500 setup; 900 mandatory support | Deferred hypothesis |
| Clean export/migration | 750-1,500 | Narrow export only |
| Broader transfer | Custom quote | Mandatory |

Managed service includes bounded monitoring, backups, restoration coordination, security maintenance, and vendor coordination. It excludes restaurant operations, vendor uptime guarantees, after-hours response, unlimited changes, and missed-order compensation.

## Support, Allowances, And Overages

| Allowance | T0 | T1 | T2 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Initial menu items | 60 | 100 | 150 |
| Revision rounds | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| Training | 30 min | 60 min | 2 x 60 min |
| Monthly managed content | None | 30 min | 45 min |
| Monthly product support | 30 min | 60 min | 120 min |

- Exceptional work: AED 275/hour, 30-minute billing increments.
- Training/content session: AED 300/hour.
- Clean menu entry: AED 350 per additional 25-item block.
- Product defects do not consume allowances.
- No allowance rollover.
- Urgent or after-hours work requires a separate agreement and quote.

## Third-Party Pass-Through Policy

Restaurant pays directly or receives transparent pass-through billing for domains, hosting vendors, Meta/BSP/messages, payment/refund/dispute fees, AI usage, POS/API, delivery providers, maps, analytics, SMS, storage, and other vendors. No third-party cost is permanently fixed or hidden.

## Custom Quote Rules

Custom quote is mandatory for unclear acceptance criteria, unknown vendor access, integrations, advanced roles, high-variance operations, migrations beyond clean export, after-hours coverage, managed operations, or any request outside allowances. Paid discovery precedes high-variance implementation.

## Launch And Standard Rules

- Launch pricing is limited to a named validation cohort.
- Launch discount applies to setup only; recurring responsibility is not discounted.
- Launch client must provide clean inputs, named owner, timely feedback, measurement participation, and acceptance decisions.
- Standard pricing is an internal hypothesis, not public or proven.
- No stacked discounts, long installment plans, or annual discounts before retention/support data exists.

## Payment Schedule

- T0/T1: 60% deposit, 40% after acceptance.
- T2 discovery: prepaid.
- T2 implementation: 50% deposit, 30% after workflow sign-off, 20% after acceptance.
- Third-party costs: prepaid or directly paid.

## Cheap WordPress Answer

Recommend cheap WordPress or a freelancer when the restaurant needs only presence, menu, map, contact details, or a WhatsApp button. Our higher price is justified only when the restaurant needs structured ordering, bounded onboarding/training/support, explicit workflow responsibility, and an upgrade path. AED 300 is an objection hypothesis, not a verified market benchmark.

## Competitor Summary

Recommend WhatsApp/manual, QR, POS/SaaS, aggregators, an agency, or doing nothing whenever they solve the actual job better. Do not quote a package without naming the responsibility advantage over the best alternative.

## Provider Cost Protection

Use finite allowances, paid discovery, deposits, acceptance criteria, alternative-fit qualification, paid overages, transparent pass-through costs, and refusal of unsupported integrations or rescue operations. Log all acquisition, setup, support, incidents, collections, and offboarding.

## ROI Alignment

Use the ROI calculator only with owner inputs and conservative scenarios. Price is not justified by promised commission savings, revenue, trust, SEO, loyalty, or payback.

## Risks And Assumptions

Buyer willingness to pay, setup hours, support use, incidents, retention, and provider profitability are unverified. T0/T1 recurring may be rejected. T2 may still be underpriced. Modules may create sales friction. POS/SaaS may be superior.
