# Evidence Auditor Pricing Review

## Verdict

Run 2C may produce a final **internal pricing hypothesis verdict**. It may not claim confirmed UAE market prices, proven willingness to pay, profitability, or public price validity.

## Material Findings

1. All base and module prices are internal scenario assumptions.
2. AED 175/hour, setup hours, support use, and risk buffers are unmeasured.
3. AED 300 WordPress is an unsourced objection hypothesis, not a market benchmark.
4. Global SaaS prices are not equivalent UAE alternatives.
5. Recurring prices include unquantified monitoring, continuity, vendor, and risk premiums.
6. Third-party disclosure is directionally correct but lacks bounded budgets.
7. No package price is proven by ROI.

## Number Restrictions

- Stripe and Telegram official costs remain provider-specific pass-through references.
- Aggregator exposure is a high scenario, never expected savings.
- All package prices, monthly fees, allowances, overages, discounts, module prices, and discovery fees are assumptions.
- First-year builder-fee totals exclude VAT, third-party costs, and exceptional work.
- The 55-hour T2 threshold is rejected as fake precision; use a discovery-trigger rule.

## Mandatory Corrections Applied

1. Classify all pricing numbers as internal scenario assumptions.
2. Remove fixed/provisional deterministic-bot prices.
3. Require paid discovery for every initial-cohort T2.
4. Replace numeric T2 hour threshold with discovery triggers.
5. Label AED 300 WordPress as an objection hypothesis.
6. Separate UAE/GCC vendor, global SaaS, and generic alternatives.
7. Require responsibility/cost matrix for hosting, monitoring, backup, restoration, security, vendor escalation, missed orders, export, cancellation, and after-hours.
8. Prohibit profitability, sustainability, buyer-acceptance, and market-competitiveness claims.
9. Preserve unresolved status for standard public pricing and fixed module pricing.

## Unsafe Public Claims

Do not call prices fair-market, UAE-standard, proven, competitive, profitable, all-inclusive, or ROI-backed. Do not promise savings, revenue, SEO, conversion, reliability, uptime, security, missed-order prevention, or aggregator replacement.
