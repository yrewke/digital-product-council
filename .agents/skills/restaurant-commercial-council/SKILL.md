# Restaurant Commercial Council

Run a business-focused council over a prepared evidence pack. The council tests restaurant value, fulfilment viability, competitive fit, and provider repeatability together.

## Required Reads

Read `references/commercial-scope.md`, `references/council-roles.md`, `references/deliberation-protocol.md`, and `references/decision-taxonomy.md`. Use all four templates in `assets/`.

## Preconditions

- Require a named evidence-pack path. If absent, stop and route to `$restaurant-research-librarian`.
- Confirm the question is commercial. Reframe engineering choices only through cost, burden, reliability, portability, legal risk, profitability, or delivery time.
- During skill-authoring tests, use synthetic evidence only and do not decide real prices.

## Workflow

1. Create a run directory under `working/council-runs/`.
2. Dispatch the five thinker profiles in parallel when subagents are available. Give each only the question, evidence pack, role contract, and memo template. Keep first-round contexts sealed.
3. Collect concise independent memos. Do not let thinkers organize the workflow or write the verdict.
4. Run anonymous cross-review, or a compact disagreement review when resources are constrained.
5. Identify the emerging direction, then invoke the Devil's Advocate against it.
6. Invoke the Evidence Auditor on all material claims and numbers. The auditor does not vote.
7. Pass the complete packet to the Chairman. The Chairman preserves dissent and classifies each decision.
8. Stop if critical evidence gates remain unresolved. Do not start final deliverables.

## Hard Gates

- Do not average away disagreement.
- Do not let the Devil's Advocate become a permanent voter.
- Do not conduct broad fresh research inside the council; send narrow requests to the librarian.
- Do not finalize package prices, sales claims, or a commercial verdict during skill-authoring dry runs.
